# Tatva Editor Guide

Welcome to the Tatva Publishing Studio! This guide explains how to add, edit, and manage content on your website without needing to touch any code.

## 🔑 Accessing the Studio

1. Navigate to your website and add `/studio` to the end of the URL (for example: `http://localhost:5173/studio` or your live website link).
2. Log in using your account credentials.

---

## 📚 Managing Books

1. On the left sidebar, click on **Book**.
2. Click the **Pencil Icon** in the top right corner to create a new Book.
3. Fill in the details: Title, Author, Description, and Genre.
4. **Slug**: Click the **Generate** button next to the Slug field. This creates the web address for the book.
5. **Visibility**: Check "Featured", "Trending", or "Editor's Pick" to highlight the book on the homepage.
6. **Promoted Ad**: Check "Show as Side Ad" to promote this book globally! It will float on the bottom-right corner of the website. (If you check multiple books, only the newest one shows).
7. **PDF File**: Upload the PDF version (this powers the "Download PDF" button).

### The E-Reader & Content Box
The **Content** box is where you paste your book text. This text powers Tatva's custom, paginated E-Reader.

* **Expand Editor:** Click the `[ ]` icon in the top right corner of the Content box to make the editor full-screen so it's easier to read and edit.
* **Formatting:** You can use **Bold**, *Italics*, and <u>Underlines</u>. The reader will preserve these styles!
* **Auto-Chapters:** If you start a new paragraph with the word **CHAPTER** (e.g., `CHAPTER 1` or `CHAPTER FOUR`), the E-Reader will *automatically* push it to a brand new page.
* **Manual Page Breaks:** The reader automatically turns the page every ~650 characters. However, if you want to force a page break early, simply type `/pagebreak` or `---` on an empty line. The reader will hide that text and push the next sentence to a fresh page!

## ✍️ Managing the Journal (Blog)

1. On the left sidebar, click on **Article**.
2. Click the **Pencil Icon** to create a new Journal entry.
3. Fill in the Title, Author, Date, and Read Time.
4. **Slug**: Click the **Generate** button.
5. **Excerpt**: A short 1-2 sentence summary that appears on the main Journal page.
6. **Content**: Paste your full essay here. The rich-text formatting (quotes, headers, bold, italics) will translate perfectly to the live website.

---

## 📢 Managing Campaigns

Campaigns appear on the homepage under the "This Season" section.

1. On the left sidebar, click on **Campaign**.
2. Click the **Pencil Icon** to create a new Campaign.
3. **Tag**: A small category label (e.g., "Spring 2025" or "Anthology").
4. **Title & Body**: The main headline and a short description.
5. **Image (Optional)**: If you upload an image, it will display beautifully at the top of the campaign card on the homepage.
6. **Link URL (Optional)**: If you want the "Learn more" button to go somewhere specific, paste the URL here.
7. **Is Active**: If you uncheck this box, the campaign will instantly hide from the homepage. This is useful for saving old campaigns without deleting them!

---

### ✅ The Golden Rule
Whenever you are done making changes, always look to the bottom right corner of the screen and click the green **Publish** button! If it says "Published", your website is live and updated.
